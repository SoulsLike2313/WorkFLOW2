## Purpose

Define a clear future doctrinal role model for `Emperor / Primarch / Astartes` as federated compute and execution roles.
This is not a claim that full multi-node mesh is already active or production-validated.

## Core Distinction

1. Primarchs and Astartes are not departments.
2. They are federated execution roles that operate across department workstreams.
3. Departments define mission domains; roles define authority and execution behavior.

## Emperor Role (Sovereign Authority)

Emperor is the sole sovereign authority over canonical law.

Emperor responsibilities:
1. final canonical acceptance into local source-of-truth repo,
2. final owner-gate decisions,
3. creator-only law authority and constitutional discipline.

Emperor limitations:
1. may delegate execution scope, but does not delegate sovereign source authority.

## Primarch Role (Strategic Operational Node)

Primarch is a strategic force-and-analysis node for federated execution.

Primarch responsibilities:
1. read safe system state and current mission posture,
2. aggregate Astartes outputs and evidence,
3. assess capability/resource/health posture,
4. produce recommendations upward for Emperor decisions,
5. execute scoped work where needed,
6. keep safe working plane operationally clean,
7. continue identifying improvement opportunities even after major growth slows.

Primarch limitations:
1. cannot rewrite canon autonomously,
2. cannot act as sovereign source,
3. cannot self-promote role authority beyond accepted boundaries.

## Astartes Role (Scoped Execution Node)

Astartes is a bounded execution role.

Astartes responsibilities:
1. execute explicitly scoped machine work,
2. deliver artifacts/evidence upward,
3. follow binding task contracts and limits.

Astartes limitations:
1. cannot set canon,
2. cannot replace Primarch synthesis,
3. cannot silently expand scope or issue sovereign decisions.

## Coordination Plane (Future Bastion/Relay Layer)

Future coordination plane should function as:
1. bastion/relay/safe exchange and synchronization surface,
2. task/evidence/health coordination channel,
3. safe working state transporter.

It must not function as sovereign source-of-truth.

## Explicit Power Boundaries

### Emperor may

1. approve or reject canonical promotion,
2. issue final direction and acceptance gates,
3. authorize adoption of draft doctrines into active law.

### Emperor may not

1. bypass repo law and evidence gates.

### Primarch may

1. synthesize multi-node outputs,
2. coordinate scoped execution lanes,
3. escalate decisions and recommendations.

### Primarch may not

1. self-authorize canonical law rewrites,
2. declare sovereign completion without Emperor acceptance.

### Astartes may

1. execute bounded tasks,
2. publish bounded evidence bundles to assigned surfaces.

### Astartes may not

1. redefine mission scope,
2. issue sovereign judgments,
3. bypass Primarch/Emperor authority path.

## What Requires Emperor Acceptance

1. canonical repo mutation acceptance,
2. governance/law status promotion,
3. role-boundary exception approvals,
4. active adoption of this draft into operational law.

## Validation Boundary

This doctrine is a target architecture draft.
It is not yet proven by full federated execution tests across a validated multi-node mesh.

3. verify no hidden authority escalation in Primarch/Astartes lanes,
4. validate recovery and rollback discipline when delegated work conflicts with canon,
